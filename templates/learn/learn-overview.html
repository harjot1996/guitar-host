A guitar is made out of six strings, each with different open notes (without any fingers pressed).

Moving up a fret (blocks on the board separated by metal) on a string will raise the note on that string by a half tone (ex. A to A#).

Having different harmonious notes on each string and playing them simultaneously will create a chord! For instance, a C major chord will consist of C, E, and G.
